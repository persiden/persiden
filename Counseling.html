Counseling & Psychological Services

caps.wcu.edu


Everyone has felt stressed out, depressed, angry, anxious or confused at some point. When these problems persist and/or they become too overwhelming, working with a mental health professional can be very helpful. Students engage in counseling to help manage depression, anxiety, family and interpersonal problems, acute problems, crisis situations, as well as to promote personal growth and increased self-awareness.

Counseling & Psychological Services (CAPS) offers short-term goal orientated counseling to WCU students. Our services are aimed at helping to remove any psychological barriers to academic success and helping students to achieve psychological health.

What is counseling or psychotherapy?

Psychotherapy is a process where someone seeks help for problems of an emotional nature from a trained professional. By establishing a positive, trusting relationship, the professional will collaborate with the individual to better understand and cope with the problem, develop strategies and/or tools for managing or reducing the problem and gain new insight about himself or herself.

Do I have to pay?

Not for individual or group counseling. Those services at CAPS are free to all Western Carolina University students. There is a fee for psychiatric services and some psychological assessments. Information about payment, submitting to private insurance or processing claims through the WCU student-supported insurance can be answered by calling the front desk at 828-2277469

How do I schedule an appointment?

For non-emergent situations, you can use our walk in service. Please check our website for the most up to date information. (caps.wcu.edu) Please stop by our office or call to schedule an appointment or to answer any questions.

If you are experiencing a crisis, you do not need a scheduled appointment time. Please call CAPS during regular office hours or by contacting Emergency Dispatch Services by calling 227-8911 and you will be connected with the counselor on call.

Since the demand for our services is typically high, we ask that you only schedule appointments that you are confident that you will keep. If you are unable to keep an appointment, please call the CAPS receptionist as soon as possible, preferably within 48 hours in advance so that we may make that time slot available to another student.

Will my contact with CAPS be confidential?

The information shared in a counseling session is confidential and will not be disclosed to any persons or organization outside CAPS without written consent, unless it is to protect you or someone else from imminent harm, mandated by law (e.g., child or elder abuse, Homeland Security), ordered by a court or related to a lawsuit against our services.
