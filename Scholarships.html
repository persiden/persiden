Scholarships

scholarships.wcu.edu


While grants, loans, and work-study most likely will make up the bulk of your financial aid package, youâ€™ll also want to consider scholarships from Western Carolina University and other sources. Scholarships are typically awarded in recognition of outstanding achievement or accomplishment in academics, talent, performance, leadership, or service. They do not have to be repaid and can lessen the amount of money you and your family would either have to pay out-of-pocket or potentially borrow in parent or student loan. If you are interested in scholarships, you are going to have to take an active role in the scholarship search and application processes; however we maintain helpful links and resources to help you narrow your search.

For more information about dates and deadlines, available scholarships and how to apply, visit our website at http://scholarships.wcu.edu.

Merit-Based and Need-Based Scholarships
Available by application for new and continuing students, these scholarships have been endowed through gifts from alumni and other friends of the University. In order to be considered, you must submit an application each year prior to the February 1st deadline.

Outside Scholarships
Approximately two-thirds of the scholarship support received by WCU students comes from sources other than Western. These are known as outside scholarships and come from community foundations, churches, employers, civic organizations, and the state of North Carolina. On our website, you will find links to many of these sources, as well as tips to guide you on your own search for scholarships.

The best advice we can give you is to Apply! Apply! Apply! The more scholarships for which you apply, the better your chances of receiving one!
