Financial Aid

Fiancial Aid


The Western Carolina University Financial Aid Office knows that deciding where to pursue your post-secondary education is an important decision, and you have put considerable time and effort into this decision. It is also important to put time and effort into planning how to pay for your education. The stress of not being prepared to pay your educational expenses can be a real impediment to your academic success, and it is our hope that this stress can be mitigated as much as possible.


The Western Carolina University Financial Aid Office is here to assist you with the process of applying for financial aid. Financial aid is available in the form of grants, scholarships, work-study and loans.
Go to http://finaid.wcu.edu for dates and deadlines.

Go to http://www.wcu.edu/current-students/financial-aid-office/types-of-aid/index.asp for types of aid available.

Go to http://www.wcu.edu/current-students/financial-aid-office/apply-for-financial-aid/index.asp to see how to apply.
Students must reapply for federal financial aid EVERY YEAR by completing the Free Application for Federal Student Aid (FAFSA – www.fafsa.gov) available only online. Prior to completing the online FAFSA, students and parents will create a username and password. The password will serve as an electronic signature for the FAFSA and other financial aid services. Students should complete the FAFSA by March 1st of each year to receive the best award possible. Many times students forget to reapply every year or assume their parents will do it for them. Students and parents must work together to make sure the FAFSA is submitted.

Satisfactory Academic Progress
Students are required to maintain satisfactory academic progress (SAP) in order to remain eligible for aid. The satisfactory academic progress policy is consistent with the university’s academic standing policy which can be viewed at catalog.wcu.edu under Academic Regulations.

Go to http://finaid.wcu.edu to see the SAP Policy.
