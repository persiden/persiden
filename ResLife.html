The Western Carolina University Department of Residential Living Welcomes You. It is our hope to provide some information to make your college experience a positive one. We have tried to anticipate some questions you may have concerning our services, facilities, and programs. If you have further questions, please feel free to contact us. We want you to know that we are dedicated to making the college experience an integral part of your personal growth and development. Again, welcome to residence hall living at Western Carolina University.


What is Western Carolina PEAKS?

Western Carolina PEAKS is the first year experience program at WCU. This program is designed to make the transition to college life easier and more successful for first-year students. All first year students are considered to be a part of this program, with most students living together in Albright-Benton, Scott, or Walker Hall and taking part in special programming geared to introduce the student to Western Carolina University and prepare for issues that are typically experienced by first-year college students nationwide. Our goal is to connect students with each other, with faculty and staff, and with resources that will help ease their transition to WCU.

What is a Resident Assistant (RA)? Does every floor have one?

Resident Assistants are undergraduate students who live on each floor in your Residence Hall. They have gone through special training which will enable them to assist you with your transition into college. Some of the things you can expect your RA to do include providing you with education and social programming opportunities, ensuring that people are following university policies, assisting with roommate concerns, and being available should you need assistance.

Where can I go if I have questions?

Residence hall offices are staffed each day by residence life staff, Resident Assistants, or Assistant Resident Directors. If you need assistance, stop by your hall office during the posted hours or call your RA, Assistant Resident Director, or Resident Director. Residential Living staff members are available at other times in their rooms. Staffing lists for each building will be posted with names and phone numbers for your information and use.

Room/Roommate/Building Changes

Room, roommate, or building changes can take place for two weeks beginning August 24, 2015, during the fall semester and January 19, 2016, during the spring semester. This waiting period is necessary in order for Residential Living to verify which students have returned for the semester. If it is evident that you and your roommate cannot live together peacefully, you should contact a hall staff member, who will assist in facilitating a mediation process between you and your roommate. If the mediation process is unsuccessful, you may request a room change through our online system.

Residents who are changing rooms must officially check out of the current room and residence hall, if changing buildings, and check into the new room. Any questions about the moving process should be directed to your Resident Director, Graduate Community Coordinator, or Assistant Director for Residence Life.

Students who are housed in Freshmen Halls (Walker, Scott, and Albright/Benton) will only be permitted to make room changes within and between these three buildings.

What do I need to know about move-in day?

You will receive several pieces of information regarding move-in day as that day nears. Please read all information completely and contact us immediately if you have any questions.

The residence halls will open for new freshmen only on Friday, August 14, 2015 at 7:00 a.m. Please follow the suggested arrival times that you will receive in your room assignment in order to avoid major congestion. Be prepared for fun and excitement, but also remember to be patient. We look forward to seeing you soon.

Are you looking for a job?

Positions include:

  • Office Assistants
  • Student Mail Center Assistants

If you are interested in any of these positions, please stop by the Residential Living Office, on the first floor, west wing of Scott Hall.
